What Regulators Actually Ask

When auditors sit down with your compliance officer, these are the questions you need to answer.

Regulatory audits follow patterns. The questions are consistent. If you can answer these, you're compliance-ready.

System Identity Questions

Q: Give me the complete identity record for AI system X — creation date, approval date, configuration, ownership.
You need: cryptographic birth certificate anchoring creation timestamp, approved parameters, and immutable configuration.
Q: Can you reproduce a decision made 90 days ago with identical inputs and identical system configuration?
You need: frozen system state at decision time + audit trail with all inputs/outputs/configuration locked together.

Governance Questions

Q: Show me the guardrails that were active when this decision was made. Prove they weren't bypassed.
You need: immutable guardrail definitions + proof that the system invoked them + audit log showing the guardrail output.
Q: Who has access to modify AI system configuration? Show me the audit trail of configuration changes.
You need: access control logs + cryptographic proof of who changed what and when.

Decision Provenance Questions

Q: Give me the complete audit trail for this decision — input, processing, output, configuration state at time of decision.
You need: append-only audit log where each entry is cryptographically signed and tamper-evident.
Q: How long do you retain decision records? Prove they can't be deleted.
You need: write-once storage with legal-grade retention policies and proof of immutability.

If you can answer these questions with audit-grade evidence, you pass. If you're saying "we kept logs in Datadog" or "we can ask the API provider," you fail immediately.
