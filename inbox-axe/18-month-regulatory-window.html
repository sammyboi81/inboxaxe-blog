The 18-Month Regulatory Window

EU AI Act enforcement starts in 90 days. American regulators follow within 18 months. This is your window to build compliance infrastructure.

Every founder reading the EU AI Act sees it the same way: "90 days to figure this out, then 18 months before American regulators ask the same questions." Right timeline, wrong strategy.

The Timeline

Now - 90 days

EU AI Act Enforcement: High-risk AI must produce auditable decision logs on demand.

90 - 270 days

First Audits: EU regulators spot-check companies. Stateless AI fails immediately.

270 - 540 days

American Notice: SEC, OCC, federal agencies file proposed rules. Industry feedback. Standards harden.

540 days (end 2027)

American Compliance Begins: Federal agencies announce final rules. 12-24 months to comply. Scramble begins.

"Companies that scramble starting in 2027 will be 18 months behind companies that started now."

What Happens to Unprepared Teams

When American regulations arrive, companies using third-party APIs face three bad choices: (1) Prove compliance with no audit trails (impossible). (2) Rip-and-replace infrastructure (12-18 months, millions in cost). (3) Stop using AI in regulated contexts (lose competitive advantage).

What the 18 Months Actually Buy

Time to build without crisis. Time to learn from EU enforcement. Time to migrate gracefully. Competitive advantage when 2027 regulation arrives and everyone else starts building their first version under deadline.

Building sovereign AI infrastructure now costs engineering time and infrastructure spend. Building it in 2027 costs everything.
