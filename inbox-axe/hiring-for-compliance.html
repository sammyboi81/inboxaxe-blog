Hiring for Compliance

Building a governed AI system requires specific skills. Most founders hire wrong the first time.

You don't need a dedicated compliance officer yet. You need an infrastructure engineer who understands cryptographic audit trails, immutable logging, and governance architecture. You need someone who can translate regulatory requirements into system design.

The Skills You Actually Need

Cryptographic Proof Engineering

Can they design audit chains? Hash-chained logs? Merkle proofs of system state? Most backend engineers have never done this. You're looking for someone from security, fintech infrastructure, or formal verification backgrounds.

Regulatory Requirements Translation

They don't need to be lawyers. They need to read regulation (EU AI Act, GDPR, financial services rules) and translate "auditable decision provenance" into database schemas, API contracts, and logging patterns.

Immutable Infrastructure

WORM (Write-Once-Read-Many) storage, event sourcing, append-only logs. They understand that mutability is the enemy of compliance.

"Hire people who think in immutable systems and cryptographic proofs, not in mutable databases."

Where to Find Them

Fintech infrastructure. Security-focused companies. Teams that built financial systems, payment processors, or healthcare platforms. They've solved these problems before.

Pay 20-30% more than market. People with these skills are rare and in high demand. The cost is worth it — bad governance hiring costs you the whole company later.
