Audit-Grade Logging: What You Actually Need

Standard logging debugs errors. Audit-grade logging survives regulators. They're not the same thing.

Your logs help developers debug. Your audit logs prove what happened. Regulators care about the second one.

Standard vs. Audit Logging

Standard logging: human-readable, optimized for troubleshooting, deleted after 30 days, not tamper-proof. Audit logging: cryptographically signed, structured, retained 7+ years, tamper-evident, never modified.

"If you can delete it or modify it, it's not an audit log."

Why Standard Logs Fail

Regulators see deleted logs and assume you're hiding something. They see mutable logs and question chain of custody. They see unsigned logs and can't verify authenticity. Standard logs capture "function called" not "decision made."

What Audit Logging Requires

Immutability: write-once storage, never deleted. Cryptographic chaining: each record signed with previous hash. Tampering breaks chain instantly. Complete context: what system, when, with what config, given what input, produced what output. Long retention: 7-10 years in WORM storage.

Some think audit logging is expensive overhead. It is. It's also non-negotiable in regulated industries. By 2027, every company using AI in finance, healthcare, or insurance will face audits demanding this.
